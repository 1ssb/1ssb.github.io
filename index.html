About Me

My preffered (non-official) first name is Rudra and my preferred pronouns are him/they.

I am a recent graduate of the Australian National University in the College of Engineering, Computing and Cybernetics. I majored in Mechatronics Systems, with minors in Electronic Communication Systems and Mathematics with a First Class Honours, third in my cohort (Major branch).

My research interests are broad but mainly centered around Theoretical Machine Learning from a mathematical perspective as well as a few other practical extensions. Though not exhaustive they include:

  1. Adaptive and Non-linear Control Theory
  2. Non-Convex and Bayesian Optimization Theory
  3. Classical and Non-Classical Liénard Dynamical Systems
  4. Democratization and Explainibility in Deep Learning
  5. Information Entropy Measures and Complexity Bounds
  6. Minimax N-player Games
  7. Applications of Martingale Theory in Portfolio Optimization
  8. Deep Reinforcement Learning in Continuous State Spaces
  9. Localisation and Inferencing using Radar Signal Processing
  10. TinyML applications for Deterministic Algorithm Deployment

I am an incoming PhD student to the Australian National University. My supervisors are Professors Ian Petersen and Iman Shames of the CIICADA Lab at ANU and Professor Fabio Ramos, Nvidia Research at the University of Sydney. I am currently working in the cognate subfields of Bayesian Optimization and Adaptive Control in Non-Convex settings.
