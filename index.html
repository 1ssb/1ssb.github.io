Intro

I am an incoming PhD student to the Australian National University. My supervisors are Professors Ian Petersen and Iman Shames of the CIICADA Lab at ANU and Professor Fabio Ramos, Nvidia Research at the University of Sydney. I am currently working in the cognate subfields of Bayesian Optimization and Adaptive Control in Non-Convex settings.

Education

I am a recent graduate of the Australian National University in the College of Engineering, Computing and Cybernetics. I majored in Mechatronics Systems, with minors in Electronic Communication Systems and Mathematics with a First Class Honours, third in my cohort (Major branch).

Research Interests

My research interests are mainly centered around Theoretical Machine Learning from a mathematical perspective as well as a few other practical extensions into real-time implementations and autonomous systems. Though not exhaustive they broadly include these ten areas:

  1. Adaptive and Non-linear Control Theory
  2. Non-Convex and Bayesian Optimization Theory
  3. Synchronous and Asynchronous Control of Networked Systems
  4. Democratization and Explainibility in Deep Learning
  5. Information Entropy Measures and Complexity Bounds
  6. Minimax N-player Games
  7. Decentralised dynamic control of Networked Autonomous Systems
  8. Deep Reinforcement Learning in Continuous State Spaces
  9. Localisation and Inferencing using Radar Signal Processing
  10. TinyML applications of Deterministic ML Algorithm Deployment

Origins

I am originally from West Bengal in India. I grew up in a suburb in Kolkata. I completed my schooling in Kolkata from the Bharatiya Vidya Bhavan. After a gap year in volunteering work, I joined the Vellore Institute of Technology for my undergraduation and transferred with scholarship the Australian National University in 2020, amidst the COVID pandemic.

My source of inspiration are my grandfathers: Manimoy Banerjee (1935-2019) was a Mechanical engineer and academic in Guess Keen Williams. He worked on various consulting projects in developing India in 1960s in Vishakapattanam and Kashmir. My other grandfather Dr Sudhangshu Sekhar Bhattacharjee (1927-2009) was an agricultural economist in India and an academic between 1950-1990. He was also a freedom fighter in the Indian National Freedom struggle and was the founder of Barasat College, his home converted to an evening college for rural development. Other family members in academia include my father Dr. Sitangshu Sekhar Bhattacharjee, Ex-Head of the Department of Chemistry City College, Kolkata. I am also related to pioneering statistician Dr. Arun K. Bhattacharyya (known for his work in the area of probability measures; Bhattacharyya distance), distantly.

I am a film buff and particularly in love with contemporary world cinema in the Cold War Era. I am an avid enthusiast of International Relations and Law. I used to be associated with the South Indian MUN circle during my time at VIT and specialised in economic committees as Switzerland though my best performance was as People's Republic of China in late 2020 after which I retired from MUNs. I had also chaired multiple MUNs. My current interests include hunting for forgotten books and international music from the early 40s to late 70s.
My preffered (non-official) first name is Rudra and my preferred pronouns are him/they.
