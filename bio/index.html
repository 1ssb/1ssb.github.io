Research Interests

My PhD is in probabilistic inverse generative modelling of 3D environments for autonomous spatial–semantic reasoning. I build occlusion-aware 3D world models from partial, noisy observations and use them as uncertainty-aware priors for planning and decision-making.

  1. Occlusion-aware 3D generative world models: diffusion- and flow-based models that infer full-room geometry and semantics from sparse RGB-D views, with calibrated uncertainty over unobserved structure.
  2. World models for planning and active perception: using learned 3D priors as world models for motion planning and active search in partially observed MDPs, enabling robust, risk-aware decisions under model and sensor uncertainty.
  3. Reliable generative modelling theory: convergence, calibration and coverage guarantees for composed generative kernels and flows (e.g. via bounded-Lipschitz / optimal-transport metrics) so multi-stage inference and planning pipelines remain statistically sound.
  4. High-dimensional inverse problems beyond robotics: applying these generative and uncertainty-quantification techniques to physics-based models and quantitative finance, where noisy, partial observations drive risk-sensitive inference and decision-making.
